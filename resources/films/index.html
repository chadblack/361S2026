    Films for Benchmark 4¶

    Here is a non-exhaustive list of films by country that you could watch for Benchmark 4, linked to their descriptions on imdb, wikipedia, or rottentomatoes.

    Argentina¶

    • Camila (1984). Maria Luisa Bemberg, dir. Portrayal of 19th century Argentina through life of the real socialite Camila O'Gorman.

    • The Official Story/La historia oficial (1985).. Film focused on the Argentine military dictatorship's system of forced disappearance and illegal child adoptions, set in 1983 at the end of the dictatorship.

    • The Motorcycle Diaries (2004). Walter Salles, dir. A film adaptation of the first person account of young Ernesto "Che" Guevara's motorcycle trip across South America in the early 1950s. Starring the always excellent Gael García Bernal.

    Bolivia¶

    • Even the Rain (2010). Icíar Bollaín, dir. A film that narrates the expterience of a Mexican filmmaker and his Bolivian Indigenous actors working on a film portrayal of the Conquest during a period of intense water wars in Cochabamba. It's kind of meta, and stars the excelen Gael Garcia Bernal.

    • Che: Part II (2008). Steven Soderbergh, dir. Part II of Soderberg's biopic of Ernesto "Che" Guevara, this volume narrates his death in Bolivia.

    Chile¶

    • El conde (2023). Pablo Larraín, dir. A horror-dark comedy film portraying the dictator Agusto Pinochet as an aging vampire.

    Colombia¶

    • The Wind Journeys (2009). Ciro Guerra, dir.te The story of a traveling musician followed by a young boy, looking for the origina owner of his accordian.

    • Embrace of the Serpent (2016). Ciro Guerra, dir. A haunting film shot in black and white narrating the loss of Indigenous civilization in Colombia's Amazon forest during the age of rubber exploitation.

    • Birds of Passage (2018). Ciro Guerra, dir. Yet another enormously interesting film by Guerra, narrating the emergence of the international marijuana trade in the Colombian Indigenous group, the Wayuu.

    Cuba¶

    • Che: Part I (2008). Steven Soderbergh, dir. Part I of Soderberg's biopic of Ernesto "Che" Guevara, this volume documents the Cuban revolutionary period.

    • I am Cuba/Soy Cuba (1964). Mikhail Kalatozov, dir. A pioneering political film by a Soviet director accounting for the run up to the Cuban revolution using narrative montage.

    El Salvador¶

    • Romero (1989). John Duigan, dir.. About the life and assassination of Archbishop Oscar Romero, liberation theology, and the civil war in El Salvador.

    Guatemala¶

    • El norte (1983). Gregory Nava, dir. Narrative of a Mayan brother and sister immigrating to "The North" (the United States) to escape the violence of the Guatemalan civil war during the years of the government's most genocidal treatment of Mayan communities. Follows the pair in their experience sneaking through Mexico, and then adapting to life in the United States.

    • Ixcanul (2015). Jayro Bustamante, dir. Drama about the life of young Maya woman whose family is tied to a coffee plantation on the slopes of volcano, an unintended pregnancy, and its consequences.

    • Temblores (2019). Jayro Bustamante, dir.. The crisis of one man's attempt to come to terms with his homosexuality.

    Mexico¶

    • Huesera: The Bone Woman (2023). Michel Garza Cervera, dir. Horror film on the expectations of motherhood in modern Mexican society.

    • New Order/Nueva orden (2020). Michel Franco, dir.. A Mexican political thriller that treats some similar themes to the popular Korean film Parasite (2019), on class. What happens when the military steps in to quell a class uprising?

    • Roma (2018). Alfonso Cuarón, dir. About middle class life in Mexico City in the early 1970s.

    • The Violin (2005). Francisco Vargas, dir. Film tells the story of Mexico's dirty war in the 1970s through the experiences of Don Plutarco, his son, and grandson, village musicians attempting to resist the army.
